About Me

The year following my high school graduation I attended Western Washington University for one school year and then decided to study at Shoreline Community College and to come back home because my band was becoming quite popular in the local music scene. At Shoreline I studied Digital Audio Engineering and at that time had some misperseptions about the role of college in ones life. I thought simply attending the college would prepare me with the skills and opportunities for landing a job in a recording studio or audio house, and there I could record and make music for a living. Although I did have sufficient audio experience and and developed skills for audio recording, songwriting, and critical listening learned from my involvement with with bands since I was 12 years old, I didn't realize that employers were looking for a professional work portfolio that demonstrated my ability to work alongside their team, and in order to actually get hired, I would need to work on building a professional portfolio by working on projects outside of school. I do still love playing and making music, but prefer doing so more as a personal endevor rather than a career.

After graduating from Shoreline Community College, I worked as an AV Technician in Seattle, WA for almost two years, and I enjoyed the position's responsibilites and the places I was able to see while working. Often times I would work in large teams setting up stages with video projections and confrence audio systems for workshop events and seminars in Seattle and Portland, but more often I would be working independently, travelling all over Washington State setting up small audio-video systems for telecomunication presentations at different restaurants, medical clinics, dentistries, hotels, downtown offices, and anywhere professional video confrence calls can be made. It was truly fun to go so many places and meet so many new faces, and to make sure their audio and video runs smooth like clockwork during their presentations. This built up my ability to communicate professionally with clients about their uses for the audio and often times finding solutions when there were problems present, all while managing the technical aspects of operating the audio and video equiptment.
